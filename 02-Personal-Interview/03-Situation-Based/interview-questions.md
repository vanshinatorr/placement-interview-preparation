# 03 - Situation-Based Interview Questions

---

# 1. What Will You Do If You Don't Know the Answer to a Technical Question?

**Answer:**

If I don't know the answer, I will honestly say that I am not sure instead of guessing.

I will also explain how I would find the solution by reading the documentation, asking for guidance if needed, and learning it as quickly as possible.

I believe honesty and a willingness to learn are better than giving a wrong answer.

---

# 2. What Will You Do If You Miss a Deadline?

**Answer:**

First, I will inform my manager or team as early as possible instead of waiting until the last moment.

Then I will explain the reason, discuss a new plan, and work hard to complete the remaining task.

After that, I will review what went wrong so I can plan better in the future.

---

# 3. What Will You Do If Your Team Member Is Not Contributing?

**Answer:**

First, I will try to understand if they are facing any problem.

I will talk to them politely and offer help if needed. If the issue continues and affects the project, I will inform the team lead instead of creating conflict.

My focus will always be on completing the project successfully.

---

# 4. What Will You Do If You Make a Mistake in Your Code?

**Answer:**

I will accept my mistake, fix it as quickly as possible, and test the application carefully.

After that, I will understand why it happened so I can avoid making the same mistake again.

I believe mistakes are acceptable if we learn from them.

---

# 5. What Will You Do If You Have Two Important Tasks With the Same Deadline?

**Answer:**

I will first understand which task has higher priority by discussing it with my manager if needed.

Then I will plan my time properly and complete the most important task first while keeping everyone updated.

Good communication helps avoid confusion in such situations.

---

# 6. What Will You Do If Your Manager Gives You a Task You Have Never Done Before?

**Answer:**

I will first understand the requirements clearly.

Then I will learn the necessary concepts through documentation, examples, or guidance from my teammates if required.

I enjoy learning new things, so I will treat it as an opportunity to improve my skills.

---

# 7. What Will You Do If Your Client Changes the Requirements at the Last Moment?

**Answer:**

Requirement changes are common in software development.

I will first understand the new requirements, discuss the impact with my team, and update the implementation based on the new priority.

My goal will be to deliver the best possible solution without affecting quality.

---

# 8. What Will You Do If Your Code Works on Your System but Not on the Server?

**Answer:**

I will stay calm and check the problem step by step.

I will compare the environments, check the logs, verify the configuration, and test the application until I find the actual issue.

I believe debugging patiently is the best approach in such situations.

---

# 9. What Will You Do If You Receive Negative Feedback From Your Manager?

**Answer:**

I will listen carefully without taking it personally.

I will understand what I need to improve, work on that feedback, and make sure I don't repeat the same mistake.

For me, feedback is a chance to become better.

---

# 10. What Will You Do If You Are Asked to Learn a New Technology Within One Week?

**Answer:**

I will make a simple learning plan and focus on the basics first.

Then I will practice by building a small example or project so I can apply what I learn.

I have followed this approach during my internship and projects, and it has helped me learn new technologies quickly.



## 11. What Will You Do If You Find a Bug Just Before Deployment?

**Answer:**

If the bug is important and can affect users, I will inform my team immediately and fix it before deployment.

If it is a minor issue, I will discuss it with the team and decide the best approach.

I believe quality is more important than rushing a release.

---

# 12. What Will You Do If You Disagree With Your Manager?

**Answer:**

I will first listen carefully to understand their point of view.

If I still have a different opinion, I will share my thoughts respectfully with proper reasons.

If the final decision is made by my manager, I will support it and do my best to complete the task.

---

# 13. What Will You Do If Your Teammate Gets Credit for Your Work?

**Answer:**

I believe the success of the team is more important than individual credit.

If it happens by mistake, I will talk to my teammate politely. If needed, I will discuss it with my manager in a professional way.

I always prefer solving such situations through communication.

---

# 14. What Will You Do If You Are Asked to Work Overtime?

**Answer:**

If the project genuinely requires extra effort, I am comfortable working overtime.

I understand that there can be important deadlines or urgent situations where the team needs additional support.

However, I also believe in maintaining good planning to avoid unnecessary overtime.

---

# 15. What Will You Do If You Cannot Complete a Task on Time?

**Answer:**

I will inform my manager as soon as I realize there is a delay.

I will explain the reason, share my progress, and discuss the best way to complete the remaining work.

I believe early communication is always better than giving surprises at the last moment.

---

# 16. What Will You Do If You Find That Your Team Is Going in the Wrong Direction?

**Answer:**

I will first verify my understanding before saying anything.

If I still think there is a problem, I will share my concern politely with the team and explain my reasons.

If my suggestion helps, that's great. If the team decides differently, I will respect the decision and continue supporting the team.

---

# 17. What Will You Do If You Are Given a Task Outside Your Comfort Zone?

**Answer:**

I will see it as a learning opportunity.

I will understand the requirements, learn what is needed, and ask for guidance if required.

I enjoy learning new things, so I will give my best to complete the task successfully.

---

# 18. What Will You Do If a Production Issue Happens Because of Your Code?

**Answer:**

First, I will stay calm and inform my team immediately.

Then I will work on fixing the issue as quickly as possible and test the solution properly.

After everything is stable, I will analyze the root cause so the same issue does not happen again.

---

# 19. What Will You Do If You Are Working With Someone Who Has More Experience Than You?

**Answer:**

I will see it as a great opportunity to learn.

I will observe how they work, ask questions when needed, and take feedback positively.

Working with experienced people helps me improve both my technical and professional skills.

---

# 20. What Will You Do If You Fail an Interview?

**Answer:**

I will not get discouraged.

I will try to understand where I made mistakes, improve those areas, and prepare better for the next opportunity.

I believe every interview teaches something valuable and helps me become more confident.

## 21. What Will You Do If Your Team Member Asks for Help but You Are Busy?

**Answer:**

If my work is urgent, I will let my teammate know when I can help.

If it is a small issue, I will try to help immediately. Otherwise, I will finish my priority task first and then support them.

I believe balancing my own work while helping the team is important.

---

# 22. What Will You Do If You Find That Someone Has Made a Mistake?

**Answer:**

I will inform the person politely and help them understand the issue.

My goal will be to solve the problem, not to blame anyone.

Everyone can make mistakes, and working together is the best way to fix them.

---

# 23. What Will You Do If You Are Given a Task With Very Little Time?

**Answer:**

First, I will understand the requirements clearly.

Then I will focus on the most important parts of the task, manage my time properly, and keep my manager updated if I face any challenges.

I will give my best to complete the work within the deadline.

---

# 24. What Will You Do If You Feel Stuck While Working?

**Answer:**

I will first try to solve the problem on my own by checking the documentation, debugging the code, and trying different approaches.

If I am still unable to solve it after giving a reasonable effort, I will ask my teammate or manager for guidance.

I believe asking for help at the right time is better than staying stuck for too long.

---

# 25. What Will You Do If You Notice a Better Way to Do a Task?

**Answer:**

I will first make sure my idea is practical.

Then I will discuss it with my team or manager before making any changes.

If everyone agrees, I will implement the better approach.

I believe good ideas become more valuable when they are discussed as a team.

---

# 26. What Will You Do If You Are Assigned a Task That Is Not Part of Your Role?

**Answer:**

If it helps the project and gives me a chance to learn, I will be happy to take it.

I believe being flexible is important, especially as a fresher.

I will give my best to complete the task while learning something new.

---

# 27. What Will You Do If You Have to Work With a Difficult Team Member?

**Answer:**

I will stay professional and focus on the work.

I will communicate respectfully, understand their point of view, and avoid personal arguments.

My priority will always be completing the project successfully.

---

# 28. What Will You Do If You Realize You Made the Wrong Decision?

**Answer:**

If I realize my decision was wrong, I will accept it and correct it as soon as possible.

I will understand why it happened and use that learning to make better decisions in the future.

I believe accepting mistakes shows maturity.

---

# 29. What Will You Do If You Finish Your Work Before the Deadline?

**Answer:**

First, I will review and test my work to make sure everything is correct.

If there is still time, I will ask my manager if I can help with another task or support my teammates.

I like using extra time in a productive way.

---

# 30. What Will You Do If You Are Not Happy With Your Performance?

**Answer:**

I will first identify where I need to improve.

Then I will make a plan, practice regularly, and ask for feedback if needed.

Instead of feeling disappointed, I will focus on improving myself step by step.

## 31. What Will You Do If You Have to Learn From a Junior or an Intern?

**Answer:**

I have no problem learning from anyone.

If someone has better knowledge or experience in a particular area, I will happily learn from them.

For me, learning is more important than a person's designation.

---

# 32. What Will You Do If Your Manager Rejects Your Idea?

**Answer:**

I will respect my manager's decision and try to understand the reason behind it.

Their feedback may help me improve my idea.

I will continue working on the chosen approach and use the experience to make better suggestions in the future.

---

# 33. What Will You Do If You Accidentally Delete Important Code?

**Answer:**

First, I will stay calm and inform my team if needed.

Then I will use Git to recover the code or restore the previous version.

This is why I believe using version control and committing code regularly is very important.

---

# 34. What Will You Do If You Don't Understand a Requirement?

**Answer:**

I will ask questions before starting the task.

It is better to spend a few minutes understanding the requirement than to spend hours fixing the wrong implementation later.

Clear communication helps save time for everyone.

---

# 35. What Will You Do If Your Team Has Different Opinions About a Solution?

**Answer:**

I will listen to everyone's ideas and understand their reasoning.

Then I will discuss the advantages and disadvantages of each approach.

I will support the solution that is best for the project, even if it is not my own idea.

---

# 36. What Will You Do If You Have to Present Your Work to Your Team?

**Answer:**

I will prepare well before the meeting and explain my work in a simple way.

I will focus on the problem, my approach, and the final solution.

If someone asks questions, I will answer them honestly and clearly.

---

# 37. What Will You Do If You Are Given Repetitive Work?

**Answer:**

I will complete it with the same responsibility as any other task.

At the same time, if I find a safe way to simplify or automate part of the work, I will discuss it with my team.

I believe even small improvements can save time.

---

# 38. What Will You Do If You Are Asked to Work on a Technology You Don't Like?

**Answer:**

As a Software Engineer, I understand that project requirements come first.

Even if it is not my preferred technology, I will learn it and give my best.

I believe every new technology is an opportunity to grow.

---

# 39. What Will You Do If You See Your Teammate Breaking Company Rules?

**Answer:**

If it is a small issue, I will first talk to my teammate politely.

If it is something serious that could affect the company or the project, I will report it to the appropriate person.

I believe honesty and professionalism are very important.

---

# 40. What Will You Do If You Are Selected for This Role?

**Answer:**

If I am selected, I will give my best from day one.

I will learn from my teammates, take responsibility for my work, and contribute to the team.

My goal will be to keep improving my skills while helping the company achieve its goals.

---
